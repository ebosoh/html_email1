My approaches to HTML Email Testing and QA:

  • compatibility: Since different clients render HTML emails differently, it is very important to test the emails across as many clients as possible. I this regard I prefer to test my HTML emails on Litmus which guarantees testing on more than 100 clients and devises to ensure that the emails displays properly as intended.
  • Test on different devices: Another point of difference in HTML rendering occurs on the screens of devises. I take a thorough testing of my HTML emails on both desktop, mobile and tablet devises to ensure that everything displays as intended.
  • Broken links and images: This is a very important aspect of an HTML email. Any broken links and images within the email can destroy the intended message and mess the aesthetics of the email. I check this aspect very seriously!
  • Content and formatting: I must ensure that the email content is error-free and the formatting is consistent. This means giving the email to a second and even a third eye in order to spot any errors.
  • Email marketing best practices: My emails must comply with best practices and regulations that govern the industry. The email must comply with the basic structure such as having a header and a footer, evading spam filters and complying with both the General Data Protection Regulations (GDPR) and the California Consumer Protection Act (CCPA).
  • Email size optimization: The email has to be limited within 250 kb but the best loading response must be just up to 100kb.
  • Continuous improvement: Upon analysis of the results of testing, I pinpoint areas that need improvement. And continuously improve to ensure that the HTML email is thoroughly tested and high quality.
